About

Both architect and engineer

Most people in my field are one of two things: visionary and abstract, or precise and execution-minded. My career has forced me to be both. I question business models from first principles, and then I sit down with the process maps and the numbers until the new model actually runs.

I've spent 25 years in and around energy: building EDF's Belgian mid-market operation from scratch, running E.ON's Belgian B2B business, co-founding a price intelligence SaaS company and growing it to a profitable exit, opening the Dutch market for a flexibility provider, and managing a gas supplier. Before that I worked in venture capital, which taught me to read a business the way an investor does. The scope has since grown beyond energy into the wider sustainability transition, because the same questions keep appearing: outdated commercial models, data nobody is using, structures that haven't kept pace with ambition.

One conviction runs through all of it: people resist change, but they are open to evolution. So that is how I work. No imposed blueprints, no politics, no "we've always done it this way". Expose reality, structure it, and let the organisation carry it forward itself.

Approach

From complexity to clarity, in three phases

01

The External Lens

I observe, question and challenge everything: processes, habits, structures, assumptions. A snapshot of where you stand versus where you want to go decides the priorities, based on impact rather than activity.

02

Deep Dive & Architecture

Measure to know. I map processes, numbers, bottlenecks, KPIs and accountabilities in full, until a complex problem becomes small, concrete and measurable. Boards decide on facts, not gut feel.

03

Champions & Implementation

Change that lives in a slide deck dies there. I find the people who will carry the new model, put them in the right seats, and embed the work in them so it survives my departure.

I do my best work for private equity funds professionalising an acquisition, for scale-ups outgrowing their own structure, and for CEOs who want an architect in the cockpit rather than a consultant in the meeting room.
