About

Twenty-five years in energy

I started in telecoms, moved through venture capital, and landed in energy in 2003 when EDF asked me to build its Belgian mid-market sales operation from scratch. I never left the sector.

Since then I have run the Belgian B2B business of E.ON, co-founded a price intelligence platform and grew it over twelve years to a profitable exit, opened the Dutch market for a European flexibility provider, and taken over the general management of a gas supplier. Some of that work was about growth. Some of it, like the E.ON wind-down, was about ending things properly. Both taught me what moves a P&L and what merely looks busy.

What keeps me in this industry is the transition itself. Renewables, storage, flexibility and charging are rewriting how energy is bought and sold, and I want to be where that happens.

Services

What I do for clients

01

Commercial Strategy

Where to play and how to price it. I build commercial plans across the full value chain, from classic gas and power supply to grid services that didn't exist five years ago.

02

Business Models

PPAs, battery revenue stacking, charging networks, demand response. New markets reward the companies that structure deals differently, and this is the part of the work I enjoy most.

03

Partnerships

Utilities, grid operators, municipalities, technology vendors. The right alliance can shorten a market entry by years. I've negotiated and managed them on both sides of the table.

04

Teams & Leadership

Commercial teams perform when technical people learn to sell and salespeople learn the technology. I hire for both and coach for both.

05

General Management

Interim or mandate-based. Margin in an energy business sits between departments, so I run organisations where trading, sales and operations talk to each other daily.

I know this industry from the inside. That is the whole pitch.
